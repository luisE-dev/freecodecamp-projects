秦始皇

Qin Shi Huang

First Emperor of an unified China

Here's a time line of Qin Shi Huang (秦始皇) life:

  • 259 BCE

    Qin Shi Huang also called Shi Huang Di, personal name Zhao Zheng or Ying Zheng was born in northwestern China.

  • 246 BCE

    Zheng formally ascended the throne.

  • 235 BCE

    Zhao Zheng assumed full power after Lü Buwei was banished for his involvement in a scandal with Queen Dowager Zhao.

  • 230 BC

    King Zheng conquered the state of Han.

  • 228 BC

    Qin armies conquered the state of Zhao.

  • 226 BC

    Qin conquered the northern country of Yan.

  • 225 BC

    Qin conquered the small state of Wei

  • 223 BC

    Qin conquered the largest state and greatest challenge, state of Chu.

  • 221 BC

    The Qin armies invaded and annexed the state of Qi.

  • 221 BC

    King Zheng proclaimed himself the "First Emperor" (始皇帝, Shǐ Huángdì).

  • 218 BC

    Qin Shi Huangdi officially ordered the start of the Great Wall of China construction.

  • 214 BC

    The Emperor began the project of a major canal to transport supplies to the army.

  • 211 BC

    Qin Shi Huangdi finds a rock with 'The first Emperor will die and his land will be divided' in-scripted on it.

  • 210 BC

    The cause of Qin Shi Huang's death is still largely unknown, reportedly, he died from Chinese alchemical elixir poisoning due to ingesting mercury pills,

  • 210 BC

    The Terracotta Army is a collection of terracotta sculptures depicting the armies of Qin Shi Huang, the first Emperor of China. It is a form of funerary art buried with the emperor in 210–209 BCE with the purpose of protecting the emperor in his afterlife.
